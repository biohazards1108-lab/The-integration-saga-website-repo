Stalker

← Back to Infected List

Overview

Stalkers are one of the most psychologically disturbing forms of the Z‑Virus infection. Unlike Runners or Brutes, Stalkers rely on silence, patience, and shadows. They hide in dark corners, behind debris, or inside abandoned structures, waiting for the perfect moment to strike. Their behavior resembles predatory animals more than humans.

Behavior

Stalkers rarely attack immediately. They prefer to observe their prey, tracking movement and waiting for isolation or distraction. Survivors often report hearing faint clicking, scraping, or breathing before a Stalker reveals itself. Their ambush tactics make them extremely dangerous in enclosed environments such as hallways, basements, and ruined buildings.

Physical Characteristics

  • Fungal growth concentrated along the spine and limbs
  • Elongated limbs adapted for climbing and clinging to surfaces
  • Sunken eyes or fungal plates covering the face
  • Quiet, controlled breathing patterns
  • Distorted posture resembling a crouched predator

Abilities

  • Ambush Strike: A sudden lunge from darkness, often targeting the neck or back.
  • Wall Cling: Ability to cling to walls or ceilings to avoid detection.
  • Silent Tracking: Moves quietly enough to follow survivors without being noticed.

Weaknesses

  • Bright light disrupts their ambush behavior
  • Less durable than Brutes or Bloaters
  • Can be flushed out with noise or fire
  • Struggles in open, well‑lit areas

Infection Stage

Stalkers are believed to be a mid‑stage mutation between Runners and Clickers. Their fungal growth begins to distort their posture and sensory organs, but they retain enough mobility and awareness to use stealth effectively. Environmental darkness accelerates their development.

Lore Notes

PROJECT EDEN logs suggest Stalkers may retain fragments of human memory, particularly spatial awareness. Some appear to navigate familiar locations with eerie precision, choosing ambush spots based on past human habits. This makes them unpredictable and deeply unsettling to survivors who once lived in the areas Stalkers now haunt.
