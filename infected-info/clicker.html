Clicker

← Back to Infected List

Overview

Clickers represent one of the most iconic and terrifying stages of the Z‑Virus infection. By the time a human becomes a Clicker, the fungus has completely overtaken the skull, bursting through bone and replacing the host’s eyes with hardened fungal plates. Blind but deadly, Clickers navigate the world through echolocation — producing their signature clicking sounds to “see” their surroundings.

Behavior

Clickers are highly aggressive and unpredictable. Their blindness forces them to rely on sound, making silence the only reliable defense against them. Survivors who panic, breathe too loudly, or step on debris often trigger immediate attacks. Once a Clicker locks onto a target, it becomes relentless, pursuing with terrifying determination.

Physical Characteristics

  • Fungal plates bursting through the skull, replacing eyes entirely
  • Jagged, hardened fungal “crown” used for ramming attacks
  • Distorted jaw structure with powerful bite force
  • Rigid posture and twitch‑based movement patterns
  • Thick fungal armor across shoulders and upper torso

Abilities

  • Echolocation: Clickers emit rapid clicking sounds to map their surroundings.
  • Instant Kill Grab: If a Clicker reaches its target, escape is nearly impossible.
  • Armor Plating: Fungal growth provides natural protection against small arms fire.
  • Enhanced Strength: Their mutated musculature allows for powerful lunges and strikes.

Weaknesses

  • Completely blind — sound is their only sensory input
  • Slow reaction time to silent movement
  • Vulnerable to fire and explosives
  • Can be stealth‑killed if approached silently from behind

Infection Stage

Clickers are considered a mid‑to‑late stage mutation. After weeks or months of infection, the fungus fully consumes the host’s head, destroying vision and drastically altering brain function. Their echolocation ability is believed to be an adaptive mutation of the fungus itself, not a remnant of human biology.

Lore Notes

PROJECT EDEN research logs indicate that Clickers may retain faint emotional echoes of their former selves — reacting more violently in familiar locations or near objects tied to their past. Whether this is true memory or simply environmental association remains unknown. What is certain is that Clickers represent one of the most horrifying milestones in the Z‑Virus lifecycle.
