Brute

← Back to Infected List

Overview

The Brute is a rare and extremely dangerous mutation of the Cordyceps infection. Unlike other infected, Brutes develop massive muscle density and hardened fungal plating that acts like natural armor. Their bodies are warped by layers of calcified fungus, giving them a hulking silhouette and tremendous physical strength.

Behavior

Brutes are territorial and aggressive, often acting as frontline shock units among groups of infected. They rely on brute force—charging, smashing, and overpowering their targets rather than using stealth or speed. Their roars can alert nearby infected, making any encounter with a Brute a potential swarm situation.

Physical Characteristics

  • Massive fungal armor plating across chest, arms, and back
  • Extremely high muscle density
  • Deep, distorted vocalizations
  • Limited agility but overwhelming strength
  • Eyes often sealed by fungal growth

Abilities

  • Charge Attack: A full‑speed rush capable of breaking walls or sending victims flying.
  • Ground Slam: A shockwave‑like impact that can stagger or knock down survivors.
  • Armor Resistance: Small‑caliber weapons are nearly useless against their plating.

Weaknesses

  • Slow turning speed
  • Exposed fungal tissue under the arms and lower back
  • Vulnerable to fire‑based weapons
  • Explosives can crack armor plating

Infection Stage

Brutes are believed to be a late‑stage mutation branching off from Bloaters or Shamblers, though their exact evolutionary path is still debated. Environmental factors such as humidity, temperature, and host physiology may influence their development.
