Spitter

← Back to Infected List

Overview

Spitters are a specialized mutation of the Z‑Virus infection, evolving to provide ranged biological attacks. Their bodies develop swollen, acidic fungal sacs along the throat, jawline, and upper torso. These sacs allow Spitters to launch corrosive projectiles capable of burning through skin, metal, and certain types of armor.

Behavior

Unlike Runners or Stalkers, Spitters prefer distance. They position themselves behind other infected, using their ranged attacks to weaken or disorient survivors before melee‑based infected close in. Their aim is surprisingly accurate, especially when targeting stationary or cornered prey. Survivors often mistake their gurgling buildup sound for a Clicker’s clicking — a deadly confusion.

Physical Characteristics

  • Bulbous fungal sacs along the throat and chest
  • Distorted jaw structure adapted for projectile expulsion
  • Acidic secretion dripping from mouth and hands
  • Patchy fungal armor across shoulders and ribs
  • Hunched posture to stabilize aim

Abilities

  • Acid Spit: Highly corrosive projectile capable of burning flesh and weakening armor.
  • Area Denial: Spit can create hazardous zones survivors must avoid.
  • Long‑Range Targeting: Effective at medium to long distances.
  • Support Role: Often found behind Brutes or Runners, weakening prey.

Weaknesses

  • Low durability compared to other mid‑stage infected
  • Vulnerable when flanked or rushed
  • Projectile buildup sound gives away their position
  • Fungal sacs can rupture if struck directly

Infection Stage

Spitters are considered a mid‑stage mutation branching off from Clickers. Their fungal growth prioritizes chemical production over armor or sensory enhancement. The acidic compounds they produce are believed to be a mutated form of natural fungal enzymes, weaponized by the Z‑Virus to increase infected versatility.

Lore Notes

PROJECT EDEN logs indicate that Spitters may have been influenced by early chemical warfare experiments. Some researchers theorize that exposure to industrial pollutants or chemical runoff accelerated their mutation, giving rise to their corrosive abilities. Their presence often correlates with abandoned factories, sewer systems, or contaminated zones.
