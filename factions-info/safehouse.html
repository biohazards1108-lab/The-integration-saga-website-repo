FACTION DOSSIER

HUMANITARIAN NETWORK

Overview

The Safehouse Network is a decentralized civilian‑run organization formed during the early collapse of major cities. Their mission is simple: provide shelter, medical aid, food, and communication support to survivors fleeing infected zones or military lockdowns.

Unlike militarized factions, the Safehouse Network operates quietly, relying on volunteers, scavenged supplies, and improvised communication relays. They are one of the few groups actively trying to preserve human life without political or strategic motives.

Leadership

  • “Motherline” — Anonymous founder; coordinates long‑range communication
  • Dr. Elen Ward — Medical oversight
  • Jonas Pike — Supply chain & transport
  • Mira Solis — Safehouse construction & fortification

Motives

Preservation of civilian life. Restoration of community stability. Protection of vulnerable survivors from infected threats and hostile factions.

Resources

  • Improvised shelters and bunkers
  • Medical kits and scavenged pharmaceuticals
  • Short‑range radios and signal repeaters
  • Volunteer transport teams
  • Food caches and water purification units

Operational Zones

  • Forest outskirts
  • Abandoned suburbs
  • Collapsed rural towns
  • Underground basements and storm shelters

Known Activities

  • Evacuation of trapped civilians
  • Medical treatment of infected wounds (non‑supernatural)
  • Distribution of food and clean water
  • Broadcasting safe routes and warnings
  • Providing refuge from raider factions

Threat Assessment

NONE — The Safehouse Network poses no threat. However, their humanitarian efforts often put them in conflict with the Highwaymen and Blackout Division, both of whom target safehouses for strategic or suppressive reasons.
