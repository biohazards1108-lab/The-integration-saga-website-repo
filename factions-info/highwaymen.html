THE HIGHWAYMEN

Collapse‑Era Raider Faction — Territorial Control

FACTION DOSSIER

RAIDER FACTION

Overview

The Highwaymen are a violent raider faction that emerged during the collapse of the eastern states. They operate along major roadways, bridges, and abandoned highways, using ambush tactics, improvised barricades, and scavenged weapons to control territory and extort survivors attempting to travel between safe zones.

Their structure is loose but effective — small roaming packs report to regional captains, who enforce brutal discipline and maintain control through fear. The Highwaymen prioritize resources, territory, and dominance, not ideology.

Leadership

  • “Road King” Varron Hale — Primary leader
  • Captain Briggs — Eastern Route Commander
  • Captain Lorne — Bridge Sector Control
  • Captain Miraq — Southern Highway Patrol

Motives

Resource acquisition, territorial dominance, and control of travel routes. The Highwaymen thrive on collapse — the more unstable the region, the more power they hold.

Resources

  • Scavenged firearms and melee weapons
  • Armored vehicles (jury‑rigged)
  • Roadblock materials (cars, concrete, scrap metal)
  • Looted food, fuel, and medical supplies
  • Captured radios and signal interceptors

Operational Zones

  • Eastern Collapse Highways
  • Bridge choke points
  • Abandoned toll stations
  • Collapsed rural roads

Known Activities

  • Ambushes on survivor convoys
  • Extortion at roadblocks
  • Territory marking and patrols
  • Raids on Safehouse Network shelters
  • Conflicts with Marsh Survivors and Blackout Division

Threat Assessment

HIGH — The Highwaymen are unpredictable, aggressive, and heavily armed. Their territorial control makes them a major threat to any survivor attempting long‑distance travel through collapse zones.
