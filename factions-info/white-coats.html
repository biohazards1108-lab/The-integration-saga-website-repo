THE WHITE COATS

Eden Research Division // Field Containment Unit

Faction Overview

The White Coats are a scientific and medical division formed during the early stages of the outbreak. Contrary to rumors and survivor speculation, they did not create the virus. Their work began only after the first infection cases appeared.

Their primary mission was containment, study, and emergency response — not experimentation or biological engineering. They operated under extreme pressure as the mutated fungus spread faster than any known pathogen.

Origin of the Virus

The outbreak began when a naturally occurring fungus was exposed to extremely high levels of radiation. This radiation caused rapid mutation, altering the fungus into a parasitic organism capable of infecting humans.

No lab, faction, or government group engineered the fungus. The mutation was a tragic environmental accident — a collision between biology and radiation that produced a new, aggressive infection vector.

The White Coats were among the first responders to identify the fungal nature of the infection and document its early behavior.

Role in the Outbreak

The White Coats focused on:

  • Studying the mutated fungus
  • Documenting infection stages
  • Developing emergency containment protocols
  • Assisting quarantine units
  • Providing medical support to survivors

Their research logs form the backbone of Eden’s scientific understanding of the outbreak. Many survivors rely on their findings to navigate infected zones safely.

Misconceptions

Due to their involvement in early containment efforts, many survivors mistakenly believe the White Coats created the virus. This is false.

Their protective suits, sterilization equipment, and classified research files contributed to rumors, but all verified records confirm their role was reactive, not responsible.
