FACTION DOSSIER

CONTAINMENT FACILITY

Overview

Site Zero was the original containment and research facility for the Z‑Virus program. Staffed by scientists, engineers, guards, and analysts, the facility operated under strict secrecy and government oversight. When the breach occurred, the personnel were fragmented — some escaped, some were lost, and others attempted to maintain containment until the end.

Unlike the White Coats, Site Zero personnel were not directly responsible for the virus’s creation. Their role was containment, observation, and emergency response. Many were left behind when Blackout Division initiated the purge.

Leadership

  • Dr. Liora Hemsley — Containment Director
  • Chief Engineer Nolan Redd — Systems & Infrastructure
  • Captain Jace Turner — Security Commander
  • Analyst Tessa Ward — Behavioral Monitoring

Motives

Maintain containment. Protect personnel. Prevent the Z‑Virus from reaching civilian populations. Survive the breach long enough to evacuate remaining staff.

Resources

  • Internal security teams
  • Reinforced containment chambers
  • Emergency lockdown systems
  • Medical labs and observation rooms
  • Backup generators and sealed bunkers

Operational Zones

  • Containment Wing A–D
  • Observation Deck
  • Security Hall
  • Lower Bunker Levels
  • Emergency Evacuation Tunnels

Known Activities

  • Monitoring early infected behavior
  • Maintaining containment protocols
  • Emergency breach response
  • Evacuation of surviving personnel
  • Attempted communication with Eden HQ

Threat Assessment

LOW — Site Zero personnel were not hostile. Their collapse was due to containment failure, not intent. Survivors from Site Zero often carry valuable knowledge about early infected behavior.
